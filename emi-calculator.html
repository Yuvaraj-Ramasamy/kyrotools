What is an EMI?

An **EMI** (Equated Monthly Installment) is a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month. EMIs consist of both a principal portion (which pays down the loan amount) and an interest portion (repaying the cost of borrowing).

In the initial months of the loan tenure, interest accounts for the majority of the EMI payment. As time progresses, the principal repayment component grows, decreasing the debt balance faster.

Factors Influencing Your EMI

EMIs are governed by three primary variables:

  • Principal: The raw capital quantity borrowed. Higher principals yield larger monthly EMIs.
  • Interest Rate: The loan interest percentage charged by the lending bank. Even minor interest increments accumulate into huge repayments.
  • Tenure: The loan payback duration. Extending tenure reduces monthly EMIs but increases total lifetime interest payable.

Frequently Asked Questions

What formula is used to calculate EMI?
The mathematical formula is: EMI = [P x R x (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N - 1], where P represents Principal, R is the monthly interest rate (Annual rate divided by 12 divided by 100), and N is the total number of repayment months.
Why does the schedule show different principal-interest splits over time?
Interest is calculated on the remaining loan principal. As you pay off the principal month after month, the remaining balance decreases. Consequently, the interest portion of the next month's payment drops, allowing more of the EMI to go toward principal reduction.
